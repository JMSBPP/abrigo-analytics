In any external-facing material (hackathon pitch, product explainer, branding, README-for-outsiders), the product is framed as:

- **What it is:** permissionless Range Accrual Notes (RANs) that hedge macro risks (inflation, remittance shocks, FX devaluation, savings erosion, etc.) for people whose currency is weak vs. USD.
- **Underlying construction:** a Mento local stablecoin paired against a token that mirrors a specific macro factor; the pool's observables feed the RAN's underlying.
- **Pilot:** Colombia / cCOP. Pilot only — the mission is underserved countries with dollar-dominant exposure broadly.
- **Infrastructure layer (abstract names only):** "MEV-free observation layer" and "options / replicating-portfolio execution layer." Do NOT name Angstrom or Panoptic in pitch copy. They are implementation details.

**Why:** the thesis is the product, not the rails. Judges / users / partners care about the hedge, not the plumbing. Naming specific protocols in pitch copy narrows the story and ties it to counterparties whose branding is not ours to use.

**How to apply:** any agent producing pitch, one-pager, slide, demo narrative, or hackathon-application answer must obey these rules. Internal engineering docs (specs, plans, code) continue to name Angstrom and Panoptic directly.
