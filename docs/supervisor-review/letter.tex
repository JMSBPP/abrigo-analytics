\section{Motivation, problem, suggested solution, challenges}\label{sec:1}

\begin{description}
  \item[Motivation.] We study Latin-American digital workers paid in Colombian pesos who carry recurring USD-denominated professional-input obligations (subscription AI services, API usage fees, cloud compute, software-as-a-service spend), a population for whom the path from wage income into productive-capital ownership has documented structural narrowing \citep{mendieta_munoz_2017,rodrik_2016,mcmillan_rodrik_2011}.
  \item[Problem.] When the COP weakens against the USD, the USD-denominated cost line translates into unbudgeted COP expenditure, and no Colombian retail-bank product currently hedges the resulting FX-pass-through cost shock at the hundred-to-low-thousands-USD monthly notional these workers carry.
  \item[Suggested solution.] We identify the macro risks \(X\) that quantitatively produce these cost shocks, validate them empirically, and design parametric cost-insurance products that compensate for the FX-pass-through exposure \citep{carter_etal_2017,mahul_stutley_2010,clarke_2016}. The intermediation product target is a contract that compensates the worker in a unit she actually spends (COP, or a COP-pegged purchasing-parity stablecoin); the implementation venue is on-chain settlement infrastructure (described in the 37-page technical companion \S4), not the economic primitive.
  \item[Challenges.]
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Identification posture --- predictive versus causal (see \S\ref{sec:4-risks}).
    \item Convex-payoff sufficiency under insurance-underwriting standard (see \S\ref{sec:4-risks}).
    \item Multi-\(Y\) differential calibration; familywise versus false-discovery correction across legs (see \S\ref{sec:4-risks}).
    \item Anti-fishing protocol defensibility --- pre-registration thresholds \(N_{\mathrm{MIN}}=75\), \(\mathrm{POWER}_{\mathrm{MIN}}=0.80\), \(\mathrm{MDES}_{\mathrm{SD}}=0.40\) (see \S\ref{sec:4-risks}).
    \item Cross-iteration familywise error and iteration-selection bias (see \S\ref{sec:4-risks}).
  \end{itemize}
  \item[Bridge to \S2.] We have run five iterations to date, all closed or parked; the model specifications, results, and failure post-mortems follow in Section~\ref{sec:2}.
\end{description}

\section{Five iterations}\label{sec:2}

\paragraph{Pair D --- BPO offshoring through COP/USD lag (PASS).}
Colombian young workers (14--28) employed in the broad CIIU Rev.~4 services aggregate (Sections G--T), with \(Y_t\) the logit of the monthly young-worker share on DANE GEIH micro-data and \(X_{t-k}\) the lagged log Banrep TRM end-of-month spot.
\[ Y_t = \alpha + \beta_6\, X_{t-6} + \beta_9\, X_{t-9} + \beta_{12}\, X_{t-12} + \varepsilon_t \]
where \(Y_t = \mathrm{logit}(\mathrm{share}_t^{\mathrm{GT}})\) and \(X_{t-k} = \log(\mathrm{COP}/\mathrm{USD}_{t-k})\) for \(k \in \{6, 9, 12\}\) months; the composite is \(\hat{\beta}_{\mathrm{composite}} = \hat{\beta}_6 + \hat{\beta}_9 + \hat{\beta}_{12}\).
Data: \texttt{notebooks/bpo\_offshoring\_fx\_lag/} (governing spec sha \texttt{964c62cca0...ef659}, \(N = 134\) monthly, 2015-01-31 through 2026-02-28).
Result: \(\hat{\beta}_{\mathrm{composite}} = +0.13670985\), HAC(\(L=12\)) standard error \(0.02465\), \(t = +5.5456\), one-sided \(p \approx 1.46 \times 10^{-8}\); robustness rows R1--R4 produced zero sign flips and the four-row consistency classifier returned AGREE.
\textit{Interpretation.} The lagged COP/USD log-level positively predicts the broad-services young-worker share at the pre-registered horizons, clearing the gate.
\textit{Caveats inherited from independent review.} The estimate hedges a correlation, not a causal identification of the BPO transmission channel; the lag effect is concentrated at the six-month horizon (\(\hat{\beta}_6 \approx +0.109\), about 80\% of the composite) rather than spread across the 6--12-month window; the post-2014 window over-represents the oil-collapse, COVID, and 2022--2024 Fed-tightening regimes; the verdict is sensitive to a single off-spec design choice (an orchestrator-brief variant including a \texttt{marco2018\_dummy} in the primary returns \(\hat{\beta}_{\mathrm{composite}} = +0.0815\) at \(p \approx 0.080\)); and the dev-AI Stage-1 R2 Section-M reframe (below) suggests the operative sub-aggregate is consultancy/scientific/administrative services rather than Section-J ICT-narrow tech offshoring.

\paragraph{dev-AI Stage-1 --- Section J ICT through COP/USD lag (FAIL).}
Colombian young workers (14--28) restricted to CIIU Rev.~4 Section J (Information and Communication, Divisions 58--63), a strict subset of the Pair~D cut, with the same monthly cadence, \texttt{FEX\_C\_2018} expansion factors, and lag panel.
\[ Y_t = \alpha + \beta_6\, X_{t-6} + \beta_9\, X_{t-9} + \beta_{12}\, X_{t-12} + \varepsilon_t \]
where \(Y_t = \mathrm{logit}(\mathrm{share}_t^{\mathrm{J}})\) and \(X_{t-k} = \log(\mathrm{COP}/\mathrm{USD}_{t-k})\); pre-registered \(H_1: \beta_{\mathrm{composite}} > 0\).
Data: \texttt{notebooks/dev\_ai\_cost/} (governing spec sha \texttt{7c72292516...751f5a}, \(N = 134\) monthly, 2015-01-31 through 2026-03-31).
Result: \(\hat{\beta}_{\mathrm{composite}} = -0.14613\), HAC(\(L=12\)) standard error \(0.0847\), \(t = -1.726\), one-sided \(p \approx 0.958\) against \(H_1: \beta > 0\); R-consistency classifier MIXED with three of four R-rows agreeing on the negative primary sign.
\textit{Interpretation.} The pre-registered positive-sign expectation is rejected at the Section-J narrow cut; this is a sign-flipped FAIL, not an underpowered failure-to-reject.
\textit{Why it fails.} The Section-J substrate does not exhibit the lagged-FX-pass-through-to-employment pattern the BPO-offshoring narrative pre-supposed; an empalme-residual bias surfaced (the Phase-1 boundary diagnostic returned a \(+0.375\) logit-\(Y\) jump at the 2020-12 \(\to\) 2021-01 Marco-2018 boundary against a \(\pm 0.335\) envelope), so the post-2021 level shift is not fully neutralized at the Section-J cut. The R2 Section-M sensitivity arm (CIIU Sections 69--75) returned \(\hat{\beta}_{\mathrm{composite}} = +0.45482801\), \(t = +4.73\), \(p = 1.13 \times 10^{-6}\), but this is a not-pre-registered candidate-next-iteration object, not a graduated PASS, and the rejection at Section-J falsifies the per-user instrument design that the dev-AI-paying population would have hosted.

\paragraph{FX-vol-CPI surprise --- Colombian CPI through TRM realised volatility (FAIL).}
Colombian corporates and remittance recipients with FX-volatility exposure on COP/USD around DANE CPI publication, weekly cadence on a Friday anchor, with \(Y_w\) the weekly realised volatility of the Banrep TRM daily log-return panel and the regressor the lagged signed AR(1) CPI surprise.
\[ \mathrm{RV}^{\mathrm{COP/USD}}_w = \alpha + \beta\, \mathrm{Surp}^{\mathrm{CPI}}_{w-1} + \varepsilon_w \]
where \(\mathrm{RV}_w\) is realised volatility in week \(w\) and \(\mathrm{Surp}^{\mathrm{CPI}}_{w-1}\) is the lagged signed monthly CPI surprise mapped to weekly cadence (carried across the release week, zero otherwise), with a small set of macro-surprise controls in the primary.
Data: \texttt{notebooks/fx\_vol\_cpi\_surprise/} (panel fingerprint \texttt{nb1\_panel\_fingerprint.json}, \(N = 947\) weekly, 2008-01-02 through 2026-02-23).
Result: \(\hat{\beta}_{\mathrm{CPI}} = -0.000685\), HAC(4) standard error \(0.001794\), \(90\%\) HAC interval \([-0.003636,\, +0.002266]\) contains zero; the gate criterion \(\hat{\beta} - 1.28 \cdot \widehat{\mathrm{SE}} = -0.002981 < 0\) fires the FAIL.
\textit{Interpretation.} Under the pre-committed weekly OLS specification, signed AR(1) CPI surprises do not produce a statistically detectable increase in COP/USD realised volatility across the sample window.
\textit{Why it fails and sensitivity caveat.} The pre-registered T1 exogeneity test rejects at \(F = 15.12\), \(p \approx 1.3 \times 10^{-9}\), so the iteration is a predictive regression rather than a structural-causal one and \(\hat{\beta}\) cannot be read as an impulse-response. Two pre-registered sensitivities returned positive significant \(90\%\) intervals at the one-sided \(\alpha = 0.05\) convention --- A1 monthly cadence \(\hat{\beta} = +0.0152\) on \([+0.0057,\, +0.0246]\); A4 release-day-excluded \(\hat{\beta} = +0.0033\) on \([+0.0005,\, +0.0062]\) --- but neither is the iteration's main finding: the pre-registered weekly primary is the result, and A1/A4 sit as candidate inputs to a possible future monthly-cadence-primary specification rather than as a rescue of the weekly primary.

\paragraph{Phase-A.0 Remittance --- Colombian remittance corridor through FX (EXIT\_NON\_REMITTANCE).}
Colombian household remittance recipients indexed against the Colombian remittance corridor with FX pass-through, with \(Y_w\) inherited from the FX-vol weekly panel and \(X_{w-1}\) a candidate on-chain remittance-flow proxy constructed from cCOP and COPm aggregate user activity on Celo.
\[ \mathrm{RV}^{\mathrm{COP/USD}}_w = \alpha + \beta\, \Delta \mathrm{Remit}^{\mathrm{Col}}_{w-1} + \varepsilon_w \]
where \(\Delta \mathrm{Remit}^{\mathrm{Col}}_{w-1}\) is the lagged Colombian household-remittance flow change as proxied on Celo on-chain activity, partitioned to attempt to isolate household remittance from third-party-DEX activity, treasury roundtripping, and bot arbitrage.
Data: Phase-A.0 Rev~4.1 spec chain (kill-criterion-bearing); window 2024-09 through 2026-04, weekly cadence (panel construction did not reach final \(N\) before the EXIT verdict fired).
Result: no empirical estimate produced --- exited on kill criteria pre-estimation.
\textit{Interpretation.} The pre-registered remittance specification was \emph{never estimated}: kill criterion k1 (intent: an X-driver fingerprint must be present in remittance event-window data) and partial k2 (data-source: the X proxy must measure Colombian household remittance, not a generic on-chain volume aggregate) both fired before estimation, producing the EXIT\_NON\_REMITTANCE label on 2026-04-24.
\textit{Why it exits.} Task~11.F Axis-1 event research returned zero of thirty peak-day flows fingerprinting as remittance, with approximately 87\% of surveyed flow decomposing into TRM arbitrage, treasury roundtripping, campaigns, and bot activity; the cCOP/COPM aggregate user activity on Celo is third-party-DEX volume, not Colombian household remittance, so no post-hoc filter could reconstruct an X-driver that was never measured upstream. The exit is load-bearing on the kill-criterion discipline: absent the pre-registered EXIT, the rev-4 plan's non-stop filter iteration would have run to a filter that appeared to ``rescue'' a signal that was never captured.

\paragraph{P1 Bittensor SN18 --- event-study apparatus (PARKED).}
The proprietary-AI policy-shock-exposed population (LATAM developers paying USD-denominated AI APIs as the downstream candidate; the apparatus pre-dates target-population narrowing), with \(Y_t\) a Bittensor SN18 (Cortex.t) alpha event-study indicator and \(X_t\) a set of pre-pinned policy-event dates (Bittensor halving 2025-12-15; dTAO mainnet activation 2025-02-13; Cortex.t milestones).
\[ \mathrm{AR}_{i, \tau} = \alpha_i + \sum_{\tau = -L}^{+L} \gamma_\tau\, \mathbf{1}\{t_i + \tau \in \mathcal{E}\} + \varepsilon_{i, \tau} \]
where \(\mathrm{AR}_{i, \tau}\) is the event-window abnormal alpha indicator at relative time \(\tau\) for event \(i\), \(\mathcal{E}\) is the pre-pinned policy-event set, and the apparatus is evaluated over a nine-cell verdict cube (verdict-eligibility crossed with robustness-consistency), Bonferroni-corrected at \(\alpha_{\mathrm{primary}} = 0.0167\) over the conjunctive family P1+P2+P3 with \(N_{\min}^{\mathrm{events}} = 8\) and an asymmetric placebo gate.
Data: spec sha \texttt{f855e036d3...b47aeab}; apparatus completed, data ingest deferred.
Result: none --- apparatus parked for the record on 2026-04-27; no graduating empirical claim produced.
\textit{Interpretation.} The slow-lane apparatus (three spec revisions, six verifier dispatches, a nine-cell verdict cube) was overkill relative to the project's stage goal of a single empirically validated risk before protocol design.
\textit{Why parked.} Apparatus complete; no graduating empirical claim. The user pivoted to the simple-composite-\(\beta\) fast-lane track (Pair~D, dev-AI Stage-1) and parked P1 to preserve the intellectual capital of the spec work, with re-activation conditions documented in the parking memory.

\section{Risk-target framing}\label{sec:3}

The risk target is FX-volatility exposure for a specific population: Latin-American digital workers paid in Colombian pesos (COP cash-flows) but carrying recurring USD-denominated professional-input obligations. When the COP weakens against the USD, the USD-denominated cost line translates into an unbudgeted COP-expenditure increase. The intermediation product target is a contract that compensates for this FX-pass-through cost shock, denominated in a unit the worker actually spends (COP, or a COP-pegged purchasing-parity stablecoin). The Abrigo Lemma's abstract \(Y_{\mathrm{inequality}}(t) = R_a(t) - R_c(t)\) framing --- present in the 37-page technical companion \S3 --- is the eventual analytical generalisation; the entry-point letter introduces the concrete target only.

\subsection{Risks}\label{sec:4-risks}

The bullets below are risks the project has already identified; we ask the supervisor to add risks we have not flagged.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Identification posture --- predictive-regression versus structural-causal; T1 exogeneity rejected on FX-vol; HAC fixed-\(L\) per iteration.
  \item Convex-payoff sufficiency under insurance-underwriting standard --- mean-\(\hat{\beta}\) is necessary but not sufficient; \citet{carter_etal_2017}, \citet{mahul_stutley_2010}, and \citet{clarke_2016} set the standards.
  \item Multi-\(Y\) differential calibration --- joint-leg correlation, FWER versus FDR, regime-switching.
  \item Anti-fishing protocol defensibility --- \(N_{\mathrm{MIN}}=75\), \(\mathrm{POWER}_{\mathrm{MIN}}=0.80\), \(\mathrm{MDES}_{\mathrm{SD}}=0.40\) inherited from Phase-A.0 Rev-5.3.1, not re-derived.
  \item Cross-iteration FWER and iteration-selection bias --- five closed iterations; meta-multiple-comparison concern; \citet{heckman_1979} as candidate frame.
  \item Stambaugh small-sample bias on persistent-regressor predictive regressions --- \citet{stambaugh1999}, \citet{phillips2014}; log-FX is near-unit-root at monthly frequency.
  \item Empalme residual bias --- DANE Marco-2018 methodology break not fully neutralised; surfaced in dev-AI Stage-1 NB02 Trio 1 boundary anomaly.
\end{itemize}
